Anna Johnning

Researcher

I use data-driven approaches to tackle complex biological questions, with a particular focus on bacterial infections and antimicrobial resistance. I'm especially interested in understanding the evolutionary dynamics behind the emergence and spread of resistance, as well as developing rapid and accurate methods for bacterial diagnostics. As a bioinformatician, I have hands-on experience working with diverse types of biological data, including DNA sequencing, proteomics, and optical DNA mapping. I also enjoy supervising PhD and master's students, as well as teaching mathematical statistics at the bachelor level and bioinformatics at the master's level.

I'm employed at Fraunhofer-Chalmers Centre and associated with the Mathematical Department at Chalmers University of Technology.

Mikael Gustavsson

Researcher

I work primarily in the risk assessment of chemicals in the environment. More specifically, I focus on bridging knowledge gaps in current chemical risk management, as data on both chemical hazards and exposures are often lacking. I work at the interface of mathematics and environmental science, developing and implementing tools to assess the impact of chemical pollution on the environment. I also have an interest in chemical policy and policy instruments, aiming to support effective and informed decision-making in the chemical domain.

I'm employed at the Department of Biological and Environmental Sciences at the University of Gothenburg. I am associated Kristiansson Research Group through a common interest in how data can improve decision making.

David Lund

Postdoc

I am a bioinformatician interested in studying the spread of antibiotic resistance genes among bacterial communities. My research focuses on identifying emerging resistance genes and predicting their horizontal dissemination by integrating genomic and metagenomic sequence data with machine learning. By increasing our knowledge about these evolutionary phenomena, we aim to improve molecular diagnostics and management strategies to combat the spread of antibiotic resistance.

Styrbjörn Käll

PhD Student

I am working on AI-driven strategies to prevent harmful chemical impacts on human health and the environment. My research primarily focuses on developing transformer models to assess the toxicity, biodegradability, and bioaccumulation of chemicals. I aim to enhance chemical hazard prediction by fusing many data modalities—chemical, physiological, and biological—using multimodal AI to gain predictive power and new biological insights.

I am also working on a project aiming to develop sensors for accurate and rapid diagnostics of periprosthetic joint infections (PJIs). My responsibility in the project will be to develop machine learning algorithms that analyze the sensor signal, thus characterizing bacterial strains causing infection.
